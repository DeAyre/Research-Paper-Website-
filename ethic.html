Ethical Implications

Can a machine have the same morals as a man if not can we make one think like one?

This dilemma comes from the famous runaway trolley dilemma in which if asked a person will have the choice between killing either 5 people tied to a track or 1 person tied to the track with a runaway trolly (exeter staff, 2020). Now although a person would have conflicting emotions trying to choose which way to go and what ethically question on which would be the best option to go with, an ai in an autonomous vehicle would only be concern of which route would be the fastest to take rather than who gets to live. AV’s may lack the moral ethics and decision-making people have, which is why many wonder if put in situations how would the car react? Engineers try to work on making ai think like a human, but no amount of programming could add the emotional and ethical decision-making humans possess. A big notch to av’s would be the cost/ ability to afford one. currently tesla (one of the biggest producers for av’s) has a model 3 with full self-driving capabilities going for $56,990 (tesla, 2022). I feel that with more time and availability the cost of these vehicles will lower to be more affordable to the average middleclass worker. also, if i am paying as much for an av then I need to ensure that what I am getting will safely transport me from destination a to destination b
