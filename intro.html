Introduction



Self-driving cars are Autonomous Vehicles (aka robotic cars) that can drive by themselves with little to no human interaction.


They achieve this feat by using sensors to sense the surrounding environment triggering computers inside the car to drive safely on the road. Even though they sound like promising innovative technology there are still many issues that affects the use of these cars such as

  1. Security aspects which includes technical issues and crashes reported on the news;

  2. Ethical aspects of self-driving cars being safe and affordable;

  3. Also social aspects of people wanting to trust their lives in the hands of software.
