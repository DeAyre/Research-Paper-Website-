Security Aspects



Biggest Security aspect of AV’s are its reported software error crashes and deaths associated with Tesla Models. Back in 2019 a man and his Tesla killed two people in a Honda civic in the state of California, California then proceeded to file two counts of Vehicle manslaughter due to allowing him not being able to control his vehicle after running a red light (Press, 2022).

This report being one of many in which the self-driving feature is unable to recognize cars, emergency vehicles, signs, and lights which results in people being injured or possible killed. Other data also shows that drivers completely disengage from paying attention to the road and willingly putting their full trust the car when it is warned and stated, “that you must be ready to take the car at a moment’s notice which could be anytime during the drive” (Tesla, 2022).

Some of these accidents were caused by human error such as one report back on April 19th, 2021, two people were killed in a tesla where NOBODY was in the driver’s seat of the tesla resulting in the car steering off the road (Isidore, 2021). In Tesla autopilot support website, it is stated that, “Before enabling Autopilot, the driver first needs to agree to “keep your hands on the steering wheel at all times” and to always “maintain control and responsibility for your vehicle” (Tesla, 2022). Subsequently, every time the driver engages Autopilot, they are shown a visual reminder to “keep your hands on the wheel” (Tesla, 2022). As the company tends to software error within their vehicles, human error will constantly be an issue no matter how advances the technology is. Which leads to some people feeling uneasy about AV’s being on the road and unwilling to trust their lives in the hands of software
